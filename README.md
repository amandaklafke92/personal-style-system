# personal-style-system
a personalised style toolkit built in conversation with Claude, designed to help me build a wardrobe that looks good and feels good

---

## What it is

Two interactive HTML files that live in my browser:

[Style Guide.](https://amandaklafke92.github.io/personal-style-system/amanda-style-guide.html)
My personal style rulebook. Covers:
- Colour palette (neutrals + accent colours mapped to my colouring)
- Silhouette rules for my body (hourglass with visible muscle, slightly longer torso)
- Outfit formulas from everyday casual to going-out
- A key pieces checklist with owned items marked
- Sydney shopping guide across three price tiers

[Mood Board.](https://amandaklafke92.github.io/personal-style-system/amanda-mood-board.html)
A living catalogue of my own outfits and saved inspiration, with annotations on what works, what needs adapting for my proportions, and pattern analysis over time. Pictures would be nice, but I haven't figured out how to do that with my zero coding skills.

---

## What problem it solves

Most style advice is generic. "Dress for your body type" tells you almost nothing useful. I wanted something that actually knew things like:

- That wide-leg trousers in inspiration images need to become straight or tapered for my proportions
- That my colouring suits warm neutrals but comes alive with cobalt, teal, and olive
- That flat shoes are non-negotiable for me, and how to make that work
- What I already own versus what I actually need

---

## How Claude built it

I didn't know exactly what I wanted at first, but this was the resulting process:

1. **Intake**: Claude asked targeted questions about body shape, colouring, lifestyle, what I reach for vs. avoid, and what I wanted to feel
2. **Analysis**: Claude identified patterns and gaps, and named a style direction ("structured ease": intentional and shaped, never stiff)
3. **Build**: Claude wrote the HTML tools based on those specifics
4. **Refinement**: I shared photos of outfits and Instagram saves; Claude annotated them with what worked and why, adapting generic inspiration to my actual proportions
